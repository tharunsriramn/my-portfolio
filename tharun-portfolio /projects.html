The Problem

When I started, vans were coming in with unreported damages. Drivers weren't flagging issues because there was no system — no accountability, no record. The company was absorbing costs from incidents it couldn't trace.

What I Built

A driver portal where drivers take 4 photos of the van (front, rear, driver side, passenger side) at the start of each shift. Photos are saved to the server with the format date_drivername — creating a timestamped trail for every vehicle, every day.

Result

When damage is found, we can now trace exactly who was responsible and on what date. This accountability system recovered $77,283 in the last year alone.

The Problem

Every morning, dispatch had to manually send a unique gas card PIN to each driver — right in the middle of route planning and loadout rush. 54 drivers, 54 messages, every single day. Receipts were accumulating in a pile gathering dust with no tracking system.

What I Built

A self-serve PIN portal built into the driver portal. Drivers working that day are auto-imported from the daily planner. Driver selects their name → their PIN appears. Receipt photo upload is built in right there, so fuel receipts are now captured and archived automatically instead of being lost.

Result

Eliminated the daily manual PIN distribution task entirely. Dispatch can now focus on what actually requires human judgment during the morning rush. Receipt tracking went from zero to complete overnight.

The Problem

End-of-shift reporting was entirely paper. Damages went unreported, mileage was inconsistent, fuel data was delayed. Nobody had a real picture of what happened across the fleet each day — and dolleys (hand trucks) were going missing with no way to trace them.

What I Built

A digital EOS system capturing shift-end time, damages, missing contents, mileage, fuel, and dolley ID. Each dolley is tagged with an ID; when a driver ends their shift, they input the dolley ID. The system compares against the previous day's data to flag which dolleys are missing and which driver was last responsible. A live dashboard auto-assigns vehicles from the daily planner and shows real-time submission status. Shift-end times over time gave enough data to track and visualise driver performance — something that was completely invisible before.

Result

Complete elimination of paper tracking. Insurance claim processing dropped 40%. Unreported incidents went to zero. Maintenance became proactive — driving a 35% downtime reduction.

The Problem

At Vanitha Transport (India), every truck had its own physical ledger book. Trip data — fuel, tolls, driver wages, income — was written manually in large letters. Nobody could look across trucks to know if a vehicle was profitable, and nothing was properly tracked. The operators weren't tech-savvy; they needed something in their own language.

What I Built

A bilingual (English + Tamil) trip calculator where operators input trip details for each truck — income, fuel costs, toll charges, driver wages, and other expenses. The system calculates profit or loss per trip and per vehicle, providing the first-ever clear picture of which trucks were contributing and which were bleeding money. Tamil labels throughout made adoption easy for the local team.

Result

Replaced the ledger system entirely. Management could now see vehicle-level P&L for the first time — enabling better route assignment, driver allocation, and procurement decisions. It also laid the data foundation for the GPS route optimisation work that followed.
